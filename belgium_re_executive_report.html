Executive Summary

Problem Statement
Belgium's property market is deeply segmented,
and pricing reflects far more than size alone.

The Belgian residential market comprises three legally distinct regions (Brussels, Flanders, and Wallonie), each with its own tax regime, demand dynamics, and price trajectory. A scrape of Immoweb, Belgium's largest property portal, cleaned to 15.254 validated residential listings, shows that location, renovation state, and property type together explain most of the price variation. Without a systematic valuation model, buyers and investors are left to benchmark manually across thousands of listings.

Central finding: A move-in-ready property in Brussels commands a median price of € 550.000, 93% above a comparable property in Wallonie at € 285.000. Within any single region, renovation condition alone drives a price gap of up to € 168.000, creating a measurable arbitrage opportunity for investors willing to execute a buy-renovate-sell strategy.

Strategic Recommendations
1. Prioritise Flanders for Buy-Renovate-Sell Investment
▲ Projected ROI: 9,6% · Net profit: € 38.325 per transaction

Flanders offers the most attractive renovation arbitrage: a TO_RENOVATE property median at € 289.000 rises to € 439.000 at AS_NEW condition, a € 150.000 gross uplift. After renovation costs (€ 600/m² × 150 m² = € 90.000) and 7,5% transaction costs, the projected net return is 9,6%. Investors should target 3-bedroom houses in East and West Flanders where renovation supply is highest.

2. Deploy Valuation Model to Flag Mispriced Listings
▲ Impact: Systematic identification of under-priced opportunities at scale

The Random Forest price estimator produces a valuation gap score for every listing: the difference between asking price and model-estimated fair value. Listings with a negative gap (asking below model estimate) represent potential under-priced opportunities. Agencies can integrate this score into their CRM to alert buyers automatically.

3. Expand Kitchen and Condition Data for Model Accuracy
▲ Impact: higher valuation precision and fewer model blind spots

Living area is corrupted in 1.636 raw listings and building condition is unrecorded for many more. Capturing structured condition and area data at listing time would materially improve model accuracy and push the mean absolute prediction error below the current € 128.858 baseline.

Machine Learning Model

Model purpose: This model estimates the fair market value of a residential property based on its location, size, type, and condition, so investors and agencies can identify over- and under-priced listings relative to market comparables.

Algorithm: Random Forest Regressor · 200 trees · random_state = 42 · 80/20 train-test split

Features (12): region, property_type, property_subtype, number_rooms, living_area, kitchen_score (0 to 3 ordinal), building_score (0 to 5 ordinal), has_garden, has_terrace, has_pool, has_fireplace, number_facades

Performance metrics (test set):

R² = 0,635 · MAE = € 128.858 · MAPE = 30,9%

R² of 0,635 means the model explains 63,5% of price variation using publicly observable listing features. Unobserved factors such as micro-location, interior finish, views, and floor level account for the rest. That is the expected ceiling for a model trained on listing metadata alone, without a physical inspection. A MAPE of 30,9% is unsurprising when prices span € 89.000 to € 2.950.000 within one dataset.
